They come naturally to believe that the wealth of individuals is truly national wealth, that the manufactures, agriculture, commerce, and labour, of special groups, are in reality the nation’s manu¬ factures, the nation’s agriculture, the nation’s commerce, and the nation’s labour. The most poverty-stricken patriot in the East End of London is apt to swell out his chest when he thinks of England’s industry and wealth, for is not England’s wealth his?
